“Her talent and lineage offer tantalizing potential for something special to come.”
—Taylor Ho Bynum, The New Yorker

This adventurous singer-songwriter, a former member of George Clinton's Parliament-Funkadelic and the co-writer of eight songs on D’Angelo’s acclaimed comeback album, Black Messiah, is a compelling frontwoman in her own right. Drawing inspiration from artists running the gamut from Dinah Washington to Björk, Kendra Foster creates irresistible jazz-funk rave-ups with a colorful sonic palette.
